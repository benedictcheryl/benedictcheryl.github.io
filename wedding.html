Our Story


We met in 2014, shortly before we went off to uni. He thought that she was a guai (乖) Christian girl. She felt that he was somebody she respected. With a common faith, shared values and similar backgrounds, our mutual attraction and affection grew. And so we began this journey together, praying that God would see us through the uncertain road ahead.

But we are far from perfect people, and as we grew closer we began to see more and more of our idiosyncrasies and imperfections. With the new pressures of studying and living in a foreign country, it was nigh impossible to always be patient, sacrificial and loving with each other. Instead, we soon found that our relationship, in spite of all its highs and happiness, did not fill us with lasting joy (rephrase). Yet by the grace of God, these times of difficulty and conflict only helped to refocus and transform us, drawing us closer to God and each other. With the support and encouragement of our communities, we slowly learnt to give up our selfish desires and idolatries, and surrender to God our relationship, our anxieties and our lives. Through this journey, we have begun to realise that both of us – two broken individuals – cannot complete or make each other whole again. Instead, we seek to remind each other daily that we have already been made whole in Christ, our one true and lasting source of joy.

“‘For this reason a man will leave his father and mother and be united to his wife, and the two will become one flesh.’ This is a profound mystery – but I am talking about Christ and the church.”

– Ephesians 5:31-32

As we head into this exciting new stage of life, we continue to pray that we will learn to love and serve God more than we love and serve ourselves – or even each other – for only then can we achieve our goal to love and serve each other and the people around us with the love of Christ. We also pray that through the highs and lows of marriage, we will be reminded of the perfect love God has for us, and yearn all the more for the day when we will be the bride of Christ – a day with no more death or mourning or crying or pain.

It is our great delight and honour to invite you to witness our wedding and share in our joy and celebration!
